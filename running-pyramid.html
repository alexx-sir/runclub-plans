The Foundation
Zone 2 runs develop your aerobic engine — more mitochondria, better fat burning, improved cardiac output. Without a wide base, the upper layers crumble. This is why we run slow to eventually run fast.
Strength Layer
Hill repeats, gym work, drills, and strides build running economy and injury resilience. A stronger body converts every aerobic adaptation into faster, more efficient movement on race day.
Intervals & Threshold
Tempo runs and track intervals raise your lactate threshold and VO₂ max ceiling. These only work well once the base and strength layers are solid — otherwise they just accumulate fatigue.
Peak & Race
Sprint work, race-pace efforts, and competition sit at the top. This is the reward for building every layer below correctly. A wider base means a higher, more sustainable peak.
