Why We Train This Way
The method
behind the club

Most runners get winded because they always run at the same medium effort — too hard to build aerobic fitness, too easy to build real speed. We train differently.

01
🫀
Zone 2 First
80% of every week is easy, aerobic running — slow enough to hold a full conversation. This builds your mitochondria, fat-burning engine, and cardiac output. The base is everything.
02
📉
The MAF Formula
Take 180 and subtract your age. That's your personal heart rate ceiling for every easy run. Stay under it and your aerobic engine grows. Cross it and you're in the grey zone.
03
🚶
Walk Breaks Are Strategy
If your heart rate climbs above your MAF number, walk until it comes back down. Walking at a brisk pace is still Zone 2. It's not failure — it's correct execution.
04
⏳
Speed Follows Patience
After 6–8 weeks of easy running, the same effort that once left you gasping will feel comfortable. After 3–6 months, the transformation is significant. Trust the slow to earn the fast.
🎙️
Inspired by
"The single biggest mistake most runners make is running too fast on their easy days. Slow down, build the base, and the speed will come on its own."
Floris Gierman — RRCA certified coach, host of the Extramilest podcast, MAF method coach · extramilest.com
