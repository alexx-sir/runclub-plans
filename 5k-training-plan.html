The Foundation of This Plan · Inspired by Floris Gierman & Dr. Phil Maffetone
Run slow to run fast
Every run in this plan should feel embarrassingly easy — and that's exactly right. Zone 2 / MAF training builds your aerobic engine from the ground up: more mitochondria, better fat burning, stronger heart. Most beginners have to slow way down to stay in Zone 2. Walk breaks aren't cheating — they're how you keep your heart rate in the right zone and actually build fitness without burning out.

The MAF formula: Take 180 minus your age to get your target heart rate ceiling. For example if you're 35, aim to keep every run under 145 bpm. Don't have a heart rate monitor? Use the talk test — it works just as well.
✓ The Talk Test
✓You can speak full sentences while running
✓You feel like you could go much faster (but don't)
✓Your breathing is elevated but comfortable
✗Can't finish a sentence? Slow down or walk
💤Sleep 7–9 hrs — recovery is part of training

This week's focus: The biggest mistake beginners make is going too fast on day one. Floris Gierman calls this the "grey zone" — too hard to build aerobic fitness, too easy to build speed. The 1:2 run/walk ratio keeps you honest. If your heart rate creeps above 180 minus your age, walk until it comes back down. That is not weakness — that is exactly the training stimulus you need.

This week's focus: Recovery weeks feel like cheating. They're not — aerobic adaptations happen in the 48–72 hours after hard work, not during it. Floris is big on sleep this week: aim for 7–9 hours every night. The mitochondria you're growing, the capillaries expanding, the fat-burning enzymes developing — all of that happens when you rest. Saturday's club run should feel noticeably easier than week 1. That ease is 3 weeks of base paying off.

This week's focus: 35 minutes is a real step up — and that's the point. Floris's rule still applies: adjust your pace to your day. Hot weather, poor sleep, stress at work — all of these raise your heart rate. On those days, slow down and take walk breaks to stay under your MAF. A slower 35 minutes at the right zone beats a fast 20 minutes in the wrong one every time.

Race day strategy: Floris's number one race day rule: start 20–30% slower than you think you should. The crowd energy will tempt you to sprint — resist it. Your aerobic base means you have energy in reserve for the second half. Negative splits (finishing faster than you started) always feel better and almost always produce better times. If you took walk breaks in training and need one in the race — take it. Arriving at the finish line is the goal. Celebrate it.
