Who This Plan Is For
✓
Running consistently for at least 3 months. You can run 30 minutes continuously without stopping. This plan assumes a real aerobic base already exists.
✓
Completed a beginner or intermediate plan. You understand Zone 2, have run club sessions under your belt, and your body is used to regular running load.
✓
No active injuries. Speedwork and intervals put significantly more load on tendons and joints. Start the injury prevention protocols for at least 4 weeks before beginning this plan.
✓
Know your MAF heart rate. Use the calculator on the MAF page — 180 minus your age with adjustments. Every easy day in this plan stays under that number.
❤️
Easy runs never change. No matter how advanced the plan gets, easy days stay at or below your MAF heart rate. 80% of all running is Zone 2. The speedwork only works because the base underneath it is solid.

Race day protocol
Arrive 45 minutes early. 15 min easy warmup jog. 4–6 strides. Stay warm.
Mile 1 — controlled. Let people go. Heart rate 160–165 bpm.
Mile 2 — build. Start working. 168–174 bpm.
Mile 3 — commit. 175–183 bpm. Don't hold back.
Last 400m — everything. Empty the tank completely.
Post race
Walk for 10 minutes after finishing — don't sit down immediately.
No hard running for 3–5 days minimum.
Compare your time to your week 1 MAF test. The improvement tells you everything.

Workout Glossary
What every workout type means
Easy
Easy / Zone 2 Run
At or below your MAF heart rate. Fully conversational. Walk if needed. This is 80% of all your training.
Tempo
Tempo Run
Comfortably hard. Around your lactate threshold. You can say a few words but not hold a conversation. Builds your threshold pace.
Intervals
Interval Repeats
Hard efforts at 5K pace or faster with full recovery between each rep. Builds speed and VO2 Max. Warm up and cool down required.
Cruise
Cruise Intervals
Tempo pace intervals with short recovery. Unlike full tempo you never fully recover between reps. The most powerful lactate threshold stimulus.
Strides
Strides
20 second gentle accelerations at end of easy runs. Not sprints — just faster turnover. Keeps legs sharp without adding fatigue.
Hills
Hill Repeats
Hard uphill efforts with easy jog back down as recovery. Lower impact than flat intervals — safer for injury-prone runners. Builds leg strength and power.
